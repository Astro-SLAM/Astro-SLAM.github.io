Astro-SLAM: Leveraging ManhattanWorld Hypothesis for Gaussian SLAM in ISS

Jungil Ham1*, Ryan Soussan2, Suyoung Kang3, Brian Coltin2, Pyojin Kim1†
1 Gwangju Institute of Science and Technology (GIST) 2 NASA ARC 3 UMass Amherst

Depth images (rgb) overlaid on gray scale images. We first attempt to identify the Manhattan World (MW) in the ISS for each image frame. The environment in the ISS is cluttered and dynamic, causing conventional methods to fail in detecting and tracking the MW.

To address this issue, we leverage structural priors from a 3D CAD model to detect and track the Manhattan frame of the ISS.

Demonstration of Gaussian Splatting for 3D reconstruction on iva-kibo-trans (left) and iva-kibo-rot (right) datasets. When there are significant rotational movements, SLAM accuracy drops considerably. We aim to solve this issue by leveraging the detected MW in each image frame.
